UCT Department of Paediatrics and Child Health Workplace-Based Assessment Prototype

EPA-Aligned | Entrustment-Based | Safety-Integrated

EPA 1: Structured Handover Using SBAR

A digitised Supervised Learning Event operationalising structured SBAR communication, behaviourally anchored entrustment scales, safety prioritisation, governance assessment, and reflective feedback loops.

Clinical Rationale

Handover represents a high-risk transition point in paediatric care. This EPA evaluates structured communication, prioritisation of sick patients, anticipatory planning, and professional accountability.

Supervisor Portal

EPA 1 – Structured Handover (SBAR)

How Supervisors Use This Tool

  1. Observe trainee handover.
  2. Click "Start Assessment".
  3. Complete structured SBAR evaluation.
  4. Submit entrustment decision.

Assessment Domains

  • SBAR Structure (Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation)
  • Time Management & Prioritisation
  • Safety & Risk Recognition
  • Organisation & Structured Thinking
  • Professionalism & Governance
  • Reflective Practice

Entrustment Progression

Level 1
Observation Only
Level 2
Direct Supervision
Level 3
Indirect (Simple Cases)
Level 4
Indirect (Complex Cases)
Level 5
Independent Leadership

Progression Logic

EPA sign-off requires:

  • Minimum 5 observed handovers
  • At least 3 at Entrustment Level 4 or above
  • No unresolved safety concerns

Digital Architecture Vision

This prototype forms Phase 1 of a scalable digital WBA ecosystem. Future iterations will include:

  • Longitudinal trainee analytics dashboard
  • Risk flagging for Entrustment < Level 3
  • Portfolio export for examination readiness
  • Programme Director oversight view
